Our Team

Must visit places

Meenakshi Amman Temple

A stunning temple in Madurai, dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi, known for its intricate architecture and divine aura.

Thirumalai Nayakkar Mahal

A grand 17th-century palace showcasing the opulence of the Nayak dynasty in Madurai.

Theppakulam

A peaceful water tank with a temple in the center, reflecting ancient engineering marvels that hosts the TheppaThiruvizha.

Thousand pillars

An architectural wonder with intricately carved 1000 pillars, each telling a divine story

Keezhadi

An archaeological site in Madurai revealing the ancient Tamil civilization’s rich heritage that dates back to 6th century BCE

Gandhi Museum

A museum honoring Mahatma Gandhi that narrates the life and legacy of India’s freedom struggle in Madurai

Chithirai fest

A vibrant annual festival in Madurai celebrating the divine wedding of Meenakshi and Sundareswarar, Azhagar fest, Thiru therottam.

Alanganallur Jallikattu

Jallikattu is a traditional bull-taming sport in Tamil Nadu, celebrated with passion and pride, symbolizing the strength, courage that happens during Pongal festival

Vaigai River

The lifeline of Madurai, the Vaigai River flows through the city, adding beauty and spiritual significance
